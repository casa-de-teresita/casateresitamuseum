### üìú A History with a Space Connection: Why the Name?

While locals originally knew the area as the *Valley of the Cacti*, everything changed in 1969. Following the historic Apollo 11 mission, the U.S. Government organized a world tour for astronauts Armstrong, Aldrin, and Collins to share their experiences of that "giant leap for mankind."

During their visit to La Paz, following official protocol, Bolivian authorities took the illustrious guests for a tour of the city‚Äôs surroundings. Legend has it that **Neil Armstrong**, the first man to walk on the moon, was stunned upon arriving at this site. **"It looks exactly like what I saw up there,"** he remarked. Ever since, this clay labyrinth has been officially known as the Moon Valley.

### üìç Location and Geography

Located in the **Mallasa** district, this natural monument is a spectacular display of nature's power:

* **Living Geology:** Rather than solid rock, the valley consists of clay and sandstone formations that create "needles" and spires that seem to defy gravity.
* **Pleasant Climate:** Sitting at **3,200 meters (10,500 ft)** above sea level‚Äîabout 400 meters lower than the city center‚Äîthe weather here is usually warmer, making it perfect for a relaxed hike.
