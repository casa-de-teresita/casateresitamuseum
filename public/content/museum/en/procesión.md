This watercolor portrays a profound manifestation of faith in a Bolivian village. It highlights the figure of the crucifix rising above the crowd, capturing the religious fervor of the participants, as well as the rustic architecture with its traditional tiled roofs. A quintessential religious procession in a town of the Bolivian valleys. The author, **Ricardo Pérez Alcalá (Potosí, 1939 - La Paz, 2013)** widely regarded as the "Master of Watercolor" in Bolivia and one of the most influential figures in the medium globally. His work is characterized by a poetic hyperrealism, where his handling of textures, Andean light, and the melancholy of everyday spaces elevates watercolor to a technical complexity comparable to oil painting. He was honored with numerous international awards in Mexico, Colombia, and Spain.
