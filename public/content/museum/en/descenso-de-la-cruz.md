### **Mestizo Baroque Oil Painting**

This Mestizo Baroque oil painting, attributed to **Leonardo Flores**, depicts Christ on the cross being supported by a kneeling saint or cleric dressed in dark robes. To the right, an angelic figure in ornate, viceregal-era garments observes the scene while holding a stalk of lilies. The work features a pronounced **chiaroscuro** and is encased in a gilded frame of carved wood with openwork circular motifs.

### **About the Artist: Leonardo Flores**

**(Colonial Painter – 17th Century)**

Leonardo Flores is one of the most prominent artists of the **Mestizo Baroque** in the Viceroyalty of Peru (present-day Bolivia).

* **Origin:** Born in La Paz, Bolivia (approx. 1650 – 1710).
* **Style:** His work is fundamental to understanding the **Collao School**. It is characterized by a profusion of ornaments, the use of gold leaf, and a blend of European iconography with Andean elements.
* **Key Works:** He is famous for his series of canvases in churches around the Lake Titicaca region, such as **Italaque** and **Puerto Acosta**.
* **Legacy:** A contemporary of the great Melchor Pérez de Holguín, he is widely known as the **"Master of Italaque."**
